Evolution ofSki Boots

Over nearly 200 years, ski boot technology has improved drastically, from its materials, performance, and binding mechanics. The developments were mainly implemented by trial and error in the begining. However, with the growth of sport and recreational alpine skiing, along with research in plastics and optimization of new design softwares, the modern ski boots was born.

Active skiers

Skiing -- originally invented as a transportation, rose to its popularity after downhill military races and practices became widely adopted as a sport. In 1930s, there were about 1 million active skiers in the United States. Along with the rapid expansion of ski resorts and chairlifts, skiing has reached its peak with 14 million of active skiers in the 70s. Today, it is about 7 million annually.

Ski injury rate

In the 50s, the injury rate was estimated to be as high as 7.6 to 10.9 per 1,000 skier days

persistent leg injuries

The introduction of plastic ski boots permitted the development of industrial standards for alpine ski bindings, which may have contributed to the significant decrease in tibia fractures and ankle injuries among skiers. However, the amount of knee injuries suffered each year has actually increased. This is mainly because the bindings were designed only to reduce lower leg fractures and no other injuries were addressed.
